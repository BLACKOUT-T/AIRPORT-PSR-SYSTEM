What is RADAR?

Radar is a system typically consisting of a synchronized radio transmitter and receiver that emits radio waves and processes their reflections for display. Its primary function is to detect and locate objects such as aircraft.

Airport Primary Surveillance Radar (PSR)

An Airport Primary Surveillance Radar (PSR) is a ground-based radiolocation system used to determine aircraft position within a terminal area by utilizing reflected electromagnetic energy.

Unlike secondary surveillance systems, the primary radar is a non-cooperative sensor. It independently detects reflective objects—including aircraft not operating a transponder—by processing "echoes" of microwave pulses.

Purpose and Safety Summary

  • Safety & Separation: PSR calculates range and bearing to maintain safe separation and triggers ground-based conflict alerts.
  • Weather Detection: Modern systems track precipitation to help pilots navigate around hazardous weather.
  • Visibility: These systems are located on tall towers at airports to ensure an unobstructed line of sight.
  • Digital Display: Data is displayed on STARS consoles within TRACON facilities.
